Phase Estimation for Speech Enhancement with Neural Homomorphic Synthesis Online Supplement

Authors

Yifan Zhang, Wenbin Jiang, Kai Yu

Abstract

Most deep neural network-based speech enhancement methods typically operate in the frequency domain, particularly upon complex or magnitude spectrograms, without directly processing phase spectra. This paper introduces a novel speech enhancement approach that simultaneously estimates clean phase and magnitude and employs the source-filter model. Specifically, the time-domain signal is first transformed into the cepstral domain via a real cepstrum analysis system, then decomposed into the excitation and vocal tract via a neural lifter. The phase is estimated by a neural network trained with a phase-aware loss function and a phase discriminator, meanwhile, the magnitude is estimated by neural homomorphic synthesis. Experiments on VoiceBank+DEMAND and DNSChallenge2021 indicate that the proposed method surpasses previous methods based on neural homomorphic synthesis, with additional ablative experiments providing a more comprehensive analysis of its effectiveness.
